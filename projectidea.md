1. Project Idea Summary
Title of Project:
Hive – A Secure Photo Sharing and Memory Preservation Platform for Preschools
Abstract:
Early childhood education is a crucial phase where parents deeply value memories of their child’s formative years. Currently, most preschools rely on informal tools such as WhatsApp groups to share classroom photos with parents. These approaches lack structure, compromise privacy, and do not offer long-term preservation of memories. Additionally, existing school management systems focus primarily on administrative tasks and overlook emotional engagement with parents.
Hive proposes a dedicated, privacy-first photo-sharing platform designed specifically for preschools. Teachers can securely upload photos of classroom activities, while parents can view curated images related only to their child through a mobile application. Beyond digital access, Hive enables parents to order professionally printed photos, frames, and albums, turning digital moments into lasting physical keepsakes.
The proposed solution aims to improve parent engagement, ensure data privacy, and introduce a sustainable business model through physical product offerings. Hive has applications across preschools and early education institutions, enhancing trust, communication, and emotional connection between schools and families.

2. Project Background and Motivation
Problem Statement
Preschools lack a structured and secure system for sharing children’s photos with parents.
Parents are concerned about privacy, misuse, and loss of photos shared through messaging platforms.
Existing school ERPs do not address emotional engagement or memory preservation.
Teachers need a simple solution that does not increase their workload.
Motivation
This problem is worth solving due to its strong societal and emotional relevance. Parents increasingly seek meaningful ways to preserve their child’s early memories, while schools aim to improve parent satisfaction and trust. From an academic perspective, the project applies concepts of system design, security, user experience, and full-stack development. The motivation is also personal and practical, as the solution addresses a real-world gap in early education technology.
